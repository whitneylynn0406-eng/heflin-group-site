Introduction

This privacy notice for Heflin Group LLC ("Heflin Group," "we," "us," or "our") describes how and why we might collect, store, use, and/or share ("process") your information when you: visit our website at heflingroup.net, or any page that links to this notice, including our contact form, our Terms of Service and Course-registration pages, and (when launched) any interactive AI chat feature; use GutCheck, our free AI-assisted home-renovation planning tool; or register for, pay for, or attend Building Your AI Digital Workforce (the "Course").

Questions or concerns? Reading this notice will help you understand your privacy rights and choices. If you do not agree with our policies and practices, please do not use the Services. If you still have questions, contact us at help@heflingroup.net.

A note on how this document was built. This notice describes Heflin Group's actual, current data practices as of the date above, verified directly against our live website and our GutCheck application's current data architecture rather than assumed from a template. Where a specific practice has not yet been confirmed or built, this notice says so explicitly — marked [CONFIRM WITH WHITNEY] — rather than asserting a protection that does not yet exist.

Summary of Key Points

What personal information do we process? Depending on how you interact with us, we may process: your name, email address, and phone number (if you use our contact form); your name, email address, cohort selection, laptop operating system, and a short answer about your project goals (if you register for the Course); and your email address, ZIP code, project details, and up to four photographs of your project area (if you use GutCheck). See "What Information Do We Collect?" below.

Do we process sensitive personal information? No. We do not knowingly collect Social Security numbers, government ID numbers, financial account numbers, health information, or similarly sensitive categories of data. Heflin Group never collects, stores, or processes your payment-card data — all Course payments are processed directly by Stripe, Inc.; we have no PCI-DSS scope.

Do we collect information from third parties? No, with one narrow exception: our hosting and infrastructure providers (described below) automatically log limited technical data (such as IP addresses) as an ordinary incident of operating our website and application infrastructure.

How do we process your information? We process information to deliver the Services you request (a Course registration, a GutCheck plan, a response to your inquiry), to communicate with you, for security and fraud/abuse prevention, and to comply with law. We do not use your information for targeted advertising, and we do not sell your personal information.

In what situations do we share your information? With the specific third-party service providers named in "Third-Party Service Providers" below — each of which processes a defined slice of your information solely to help us operate the Services — and otherwise only as described in "When and With Whom Do We Share Your Personal Information?"

How do we keep your information safe? We rely on the security practices of reputable, established infrastructure providers (Stripe, GitHub, Vercel, Supabase, Anthropic) and take reasonable precautions of our own. However, no method of transmission or storage is 100% secure, and we cannot guarantee absolute security. See "How Do We Keep Your Information Safe?"

Do we make an anonymity claim? No. We do not claim that any information you submit to us — including any free-text field, photo, or (once launched) AI chat message — is anonymous. If you type or upload identifying information, it will be present in what we store. See "What Are Your Privacy Rights?"

How do you exercise your rights? Email us at help@heflingroup.net. We will consider and act on any request consistent with applicable law. See "How Can You Review, Update, or Delete the Data We Collect From You?"

1. What Information Do We Collect?

We collect different information depending on which part of the Services you use. Each flow is described separately below so you can see exactly what applies to you.

1.1 Site contact form (heflingroup.net homepage). When you submit our "Reach out for a discovery call" form, we collect the fields you enter: name (required), email address (required), phone number (optional), and your message. This form is processed by our third-party form provider, Web3Forms (see Section 2), which relays your submission to our inbox by email. A hidden "honeypot" field exists solely to catch automated bot submissions; it is not visible to, and does not collect anything from, a real visitor.

Live-verified 2026-07-14: we independently re-fetched and inspected the live heflingroup.net homepage before writing this notice. It contains no analytics script, no advertising pixel, and no tracking cookie of any kind — only the contact form described above, a link to Whitney Heflin's LinkedIn profile, and a preconnect to Google's font-hosting servers (see Section 6).

1.2 Course registration ("Building Your AI Digital Workforce"). Free info-session registration collects: name and email address (both required), and an optional short answer to "What are you hoping to build?"

Paid Course registration collects: name and email address (both required); your cohort selection; a required short answer to "What do you most want to build?"; your laptop operating system (Windows/Mac); and your affirmative checkbox agreement to our Terms of Participation. These fields are collected through Tally, a third-party form provider (see Section 2), which records your submission together with a timestamp.

Course payment is processed entirely by Stripe, Inc. Heflin Group never collects, stores, or has access to your payment-card number, expiration date, or security code — Stripe alone processes and stores that data, under its own terms and privacy policy. We do receive, from Stripe, confirmation that an invoice was paid, the amount, and the billing name/email associated with the payment (as needed for our own recordkeeping and tax-compliance obligations).

1.3 GutCheck (our free renovation-planning tool). When you use GutCheck, our intake form collects: your email address (required — used so you can find your plan again later; it is not currently used to send you anything, and is not required to create an account, because GutCheck does not currently have accounts or logins); your ZIP code; your project details (the type of work you're planning, your rough budget, your stylistic preference, your subfloor type, your home's age, your bathroom level, and — where relevant — toilet position); and, where you choose to provide them, up to four photographs of your project area.

We also automatically log a cryptographically hashed version of your IP address (not your raw IP address) — used solely to apply a daily per-visitor usage limit and to detect abuse. Your raw IP address is not stored in this table.

The plan GutCheck generates for you (including the intake details above and, if provided, your photos) is stored against a unique, non-guessable plan identifier, so that you and anyone you share the plan link with can view it again later.

Your photographs are sent to Anthropic, PBC (the provider of the Claude AI model that powers GutCheck's plan generation) as part of generating your plan. See Section 2.

1.4 AI chat feature. Heflin Group has approved a design for an interactive AI chat feature on heflingroup.net, but this feature is not live as of the date of this notice. See Section 7 for what will apply once (and if) it launches.

1.5 Information we do not collect. We do not require you to create an account or log in to use GutCheck, submit the contact form, or register for the free info session. We do not run advertising trackers or sell audience data to advertisers. We do not knowingly collect Social Security numbers, driver's license numbers, financial account numbers (beyond what Stripe processes on our behalf, to which we have no access), or health information.

7. The AI Chat Feature (Not Yet Live)

Heflin Group has approved, but not yet launched, an interactive AI chat feature for heflingroup.net. This section describes what will apply once it launches — it does not describe a feature you can use today. We are including it here, rather than waiting to publish it later, so that the practice is on the record and reviewable before the feature goes live.

If and when the chat feature launches, the following design has been approved:

No automatic collection. The chat tool will not collect your IP address, require a login, or use browser fingerprinting. A random session token will be used only to keep a single conversation coherent.

90-day raw log retention. Raw conversation logs will be kept for approximately 90 days and then deleted. Aggregate, non-identifying trend statistics (for example, counts of common question topics) may be retained longer.

Optional follow-up email field. If the chat interface offers an optional field to leave your email for a follow-up, providing it will be voluntary, and it will be used solely for that one follow-up — not added to a marketing list — unless you separately and affirmatively opt in to marketing communications.

No anonymity guarantee. Because the chat is free-text, it will contain whatever you choose to type, which may include your name or other identifying details if you include them. We will not represent that chat logs are anonymous.

Anthropic as processor. Every message will be processed by Anthropic, PBC to generate a response, under Anthropic's own privacy policy and terms.

This feature will not go live until two conditions are both met: (1) a Kentucky attorney has reviewed the governing terms and this notice as they apply to the chat feature; and (2) the 90-day automated deletion mechanism described above has actually been built and verified in our backend — we will not publish a deletion promise that no working mechanism yet backs. As of the date of this notice, neither condition has been met, which is precisely why the feature is not live.

12. Controls for Do-Not-Track Features

Most web browsers, and some mobile operating systems, include a Do-Not-Track ("DNT") feature or setting you can activate to signal a preference not to have your online browsing activity monitored and collected. No uniform technical standard for recognizing and honoring DNT signals has been finalized industry-wide. Because of this, we do not currently respond to DNT browser signals or any similar automated mechanism. This is a low-stakes disclosure at present given that, as described in Section 6, our website does not deploy tracking technologies for DNT to meaningfully govern — but we are including this section for completeness and because California law requires us to state our DNT practice regardless.
